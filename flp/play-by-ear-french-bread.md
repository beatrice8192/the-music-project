At the moment when I am making this set of play-by-ear, my composition skill is 60%, software skill is 20%, while performance skill is 10%. I learned piano with instructors from grade 2 to 10, and acoustic guitar for one school term.

Composition aspect: The music of Melty Blood are rocky, sentimental, and suitable to be repeated forever for a 2D fighting game. I cheated the play-by-ear by referencing other people's play-by-ear copies on 01, 02, 03, 04, 06, 07, cleaning them up and adding my improvises. The rest of them were made from scratch.

Software aspect: As anyone can tell that I only use two patterns per music piece, one for the intro, one for the main loop.

My preferred instruments are piano, acoustic guitar, rock guitar, rock organ. I avoided using strings as much as possible as I don't have the skills to make it sound realistic in the synthesizer neither do I have the skill to perform it.

### Assignments

1. Coarse grain: Cover full length of the song.
    1. Detecting the song's speed and pitch.
    1. Stretch the song to separate a few tracks.
    1. Transcribe lead track.
    1. Transcribe bass track from stretched audio.
    1. Duplicate a drum track from the composer's previous midi works.
1. Fine grain.
    1. Practise composing for 30 minutes without listening for every session.
    1. Define rhythms.
    1. Compose chord/rhythm track because you can't hear this clearly as the bass track.
    1. Compose opening and ending.
    1. Fine grain bass track with improvisation.
        1. Match bass track with rhythms/emphasis.
        1. Add energy with techniques such as octaves.
        1. The bass composition will drift away from original composition as expected.
        1. Cross listening to address dissonance.
    1. Fine grain chord/rhythm track.
        1. Usually there are multiple chord rhythm tracks. They need to coordinate with each other.
        1. The common instruments are acoustic guitar, rock guitar, piano.
        1. Cross listening to address dissonance.
    1. Fine grain lead track.
        1. Add intervals.
        1. Add glissando.
        1. Add retention.
        1. Pay attention to sentences/pauses.
        1. Lead track can be doubled up with multiple instruments. Example: under-night-in-birth: Linne, Olie, Yuzuriha, Mika.
    1. Fine grain drum track with improvisation.
        1. Separate drum instruments to multiple tracks for volume control. Different plugins yield different volume.
        1. Match drum track with rhythms/emphasis.
    1. Add decorations such as xylophone, triangle, cymbal, bell.
1. Integration.
    1. Tune instruments (may become a separate large project).
    1. Mix multiple versions of the song.
    1. Try different instruments and different sentiment of the song.

### Preparations

1. Revisit the composer's previous midi works.
1. Create libraries.
    1. Create drum patterns library.
    1. Create chords/rhythms library.
    1. Create glissando library.
1. Create automations.
    1. Speed detection.
    1. Pitch detection.
    1. Bass transcription (advanced).
    1. Sort songs based on bass transcription (advanced).
    1. Normalizing (for compilation instead of composition).
1. Read books on music theory and fine grain terms.
1. Learn instruments performance techniques to avoid rudimentary mistakes in composition.
